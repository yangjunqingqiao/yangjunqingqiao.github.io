Gaussian Normalization (2018)

Exploring the results of using a gaussian distribution in place of z-score for batch normalization.

For the final project of a neural networks course, inspired by the idea of copula normalization, I investigated the effectiveness of normalizing the feedforward layers of a neural network using a gaussian distribution. Ultimately the naive implementation of gaussian batch normalization was found to be too computationally expensive (backpropagation through the normalizing function required computing logs) and performed no better than regular z-score batch normalization on a benchmark dataset.

Neural Networks Survey Paper (2017)

Analysis of the dichotomy between theory and application of neural networks.

An unpublished paper I worked on in the summer of 2017 during REUMass Amherst under the mentorship of Professor David Barrington and David Tench. It explores what insights complexity, circuit, and graph theory can provide about the computational limitations of neural networks.
